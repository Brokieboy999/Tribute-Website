A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

APJ Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015, in Shillong, Meghalaya, India. Known as the "People's President" and the "Missile Man of India," Kalam made significant contributions to India's space and defense programs.


Abdul Kalam hailed from a modest background and faced numerous hardships during his childhood. He came from a family of boat owners and had to support his family by selling newspapers at an early age. However, he was a dedicated student with a keen interest in science and mathematics. Despite financial challenges, Kalam pursued his education and earned a degree in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later completed his aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.


Kalam's career began in 1958 when he joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO), India's premier defense research institute. He played a significant role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle program and was responsible for the successful deployment of the satellite Rohini-1 in 1980. Later, he led India's guided missile development program, resulting in the successful testing of several missiles such as Agni and Prithvi.


Apart from his contributions to India's defense and space programs, Kalam was a passionate advocate for science and education. He strongly believed in the power of technology and science to uplift the lives of people and transform nations. Kalam played a pivotal role in developing India's nuclear capabilities and was a key figure in the country's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.


In 2002, Abdul Kalam was elected as the President of India, becoming the first scientist to hold the highest office in the country. During his presidency, Kalam focused on connecting with the youth and inspiring them to pursue scientific research and innovation. He was known for his humility, accessibility, and his ability to connect with people from all walks of life.


After his presidency, Kalam continued to be actively involved in various educational and scientific initiatives. He visited schools, colleges, and universities, delivering motivational speeches to inspire the younger generation. Kalam emphasized the importance of imparting practical skills and values in education to foster creativity, innovation, and social responsibility.
